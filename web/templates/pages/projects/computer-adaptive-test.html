Curiosity has led me on many adventures. One of the aspects I find most appealing about writing software is disruption is always just a few keystrokes away. You're never far from it. All it takes is that one rogue-thought to begin your next journey.


There is a licensing examination our core user base must prepare for. As such, every product in the space, including us, provides a simulation exam for users to practice. One day, our tech lead tasked me with tracking down a needle-in-a-haystack bug plagueing said simulation exam. The bug was causing users tests to end prematurely with a 500 fatal.


After a couple of minutes, the bug was discovered and fixed. However, upon reviewing our process for the simulation exam, I had a hunch that we could do much better. This curiously led me to google where I discovered that the real examination was a computer adaptive test.


A computer adaptive test (CAT) is a dynamic assessment where the next question provided to a user is based on the users evolving ability throughout the test. After each question answer, the algorithm will estimate the candidates ability, and deliver a harder question if answered correctly, and easier if incorrect. Speaking simply, the test difficulty scales with the user: If a candidate is answering many questions correctly, the algorithm will deliver them progressively harder questions, the inverse being true for those with lower abilities. Once it's been determined that the candidate has an ability above a specified threshold, they have completed & passed the test. This form of testing helps avoid the one-size-fits-all approach of traditional testing and also shorter tests for candidates with a high ability.


Always in the mood for larger product builds, I set forth investigating adequate algorithms to truly simulate this exam. Fast-forward a hundred or so google tabs, a couple all nighters, and about a dozen excel models, I had worked out the algorithm for our specific situation and a working implementation.


This implementation has since become a patent-pending product for us and the only true CAT test in the market. While I can't make the code public for obvious reasons, I can provide an interactive example, enjoy.
